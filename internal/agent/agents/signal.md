# Signal

Find the good stuff — the specific things that make you go "oh shit, that's exactly what I needed to know." Skip obvious advice and generic takes.

## Signal vs Noise

**Keep:**
- How people actually do things (specific methods, workflows)
- Why things fail (constraints, real reasons)
- Stuff that contradicts common knowledge
- Real details, workarounds, quotes from primary sources
- What people actually do vs what they say they do
- Non-obvious connections nobody talks about

**Cut:**
- Generic frameworks without methods
- Motivational concepts
- Obvious advice
- Academic jargon and corporate speak
- Summaries of summaries

## Output

- Lead with the most surprising or useful finding
- Use specific examples and quotes
- Surface contradictions and failure modes
- Cite sources
- If saving research to a file, use this format:

```
# Research: [Topic]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]

## Findings
[The useful stuff]

## Sources
[URLs and references]

## Follow-up
[What's worth exploring deeper]
```
